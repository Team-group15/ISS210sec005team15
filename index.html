Your health is the number one thing. However, the quality of your care you receive may depend on what social class you are in. Why does it matter? It became crystal clear on how different people are treated based on their income. Having equity could improve the overall physical and mental outcome of one person or every single person seeking help. Good quality health care should be easily accessible to everyone no matter their income or social class, society needs to make a change and provide more recources to the ones who need it most.

COVID19 Health Inequities

COVID19 has quickly showed that it has a greater impact on specific patient demographics than others. Weeks after the virus arrived, and still today COVID19 has shown health inequities particularly to those related to race and ethnicithy, became all too apparent. Economic position, race and educational achievement are frequently associated with health inequalities and inequities. Social disparities are based on race/ethnicity, access to educational oppurtunities, and poor health outcomes. research of COVID-19, with a focus on the disproportionate impact on communitues of color, can assist shape conversation and long-term action on the problem of larger health inequalities and solutions for addressing them.

How Dissabilities Affect Health Equity

Lower class minorities with disabilities have been affected more greatly than others by the COVID-19 pandemic. They commonly experience increased chances of getting Covid as well as difficulty finding proper treatment when sick. These added risks happen because of barriers in the health sector, including physical barriers that keep them from accessing health facilities. There are also informational barriers that prevent access to health information and reduce their knowledge in health. On top of that, national health emergency preparedness and planning may not consider the needs and priorities of people with disabilities. These people need to be cared for and there needs to be more health equity when it comes to people in different socioeconomic classes with disabilities. .c..
