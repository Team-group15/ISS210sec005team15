About

Your health is the number one thing. However, the quality of your care you receive may depend on what social class you are in. Why does it matter? To raise awareness to the people and most importantly the government. It’s a pretty known fact that healthcare is getting more and more expensive as the years go on. Insurances are covered less each year because of it, people who are in lower class aren’t able to afford the care they need. It's the difference between finding a good doctor and getting great care. During the pandemic, places who are in poverty were receiving the least help and contributing to the spread the most because of lack of medical care and resources. It became crystal clear on how different people are treated based on their income. Having equity could improve the overall physical and mental outcome of one person or every single person seeking help.

Health Equity Framework AAFP Efforts

This image represents improving health equity and reducing health disparities. Their number one priority is building health organizations' capacity and the workforce. Advanced language access and health literacy. They want to have community partnerships within a culture of diversity. The image points out that integrating and implementing the priorities will be essential to eliminating barriers to health equity with the community's help. A quote from the website is “One area the AAFP is actively pursuing that we wished was more clearly reflected in the framework is an intention to embed antiracism as a way to address health disparities and improve equity,” Savoy said.
